Mangroves

The lush, mangrove farms located in sitio Pantar, barangay Villa Hermosa and barangay Las-ud cover an area of almost two hectares and 3.72 hectares, respecively. FEd by buaya River and the irrigation system of barangay Quinsoriano, they are home to a number of marine lives like tilapia, bangus(milkfish), malaga(siganid),kapiged(siganid), barangen (siganid), swordfish, oyster , pawn shrimp , crab, clam and sea grass. The mangrove projects were initiated by the Local Government Unit of Sta. Cruz in 2013 with the effort and cooperation of barangay and support from the bureau of Fisheries and Aquatic Resources (BFAR) and the Tan Yan Kee Foundation.

Roads leading to the farms are concreted except for the five-minute (approximately) trek which would require tourist to walk on foot on dirt paths. It is accessible to tricycles and private vehiclces. There are no facilities yet on the farms because the properties sorrounding them are privately owned. LGU Sta. Cruz included PPA's for the improvement of emerging and potential tourism sites in the Executive - Legislative Agenda 2022-2024 including the two mangrove farms.
